How do farm animals emit greenhouse gases?

Key Ideas:

  • Some farm animals (cows, sheeps) are emitting methane as a result of their digestion.
  • About 30% of global methane emissions come from this source.
  • There does not seem to be a way to eliminate these emissions while still keeping them as farm animals.

When comparing the climate impact of animal foods, you might have noticed, that the one of cow products is significantly higher than the one of pigs or chicken for example. Part of that is attributable to the stems from the difference in energy efficiency which depends on body size (more on that HERE). Another part though is the digestion system of cattle and other ruminating animals (sheep for example).

Rumination has evolved to be able to digest very fiber rich plants, like gras, which single-chamber stomach animals cannot utilise. The end of this process involves methane producing microbes. The cow then burps this methane into the atmosphere.

The problem becomes more apparent considering the sheer scale of livestock raised for meat production throughout the world. A single cow produces between 154 to 264 pounds of methane gas per year. Not counting for the emissions of any other livestock, 1.5 billion cattle, raised specifically for meat production worldwide, emit at least 231 billion pounds of methane into the methane into the atmosphere each year.
